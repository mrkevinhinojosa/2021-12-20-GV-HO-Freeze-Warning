Important Homeowner Information

The National Weather Service has issued a freeze warning in the Fresno area from 11 p.m. on Friday, December 10th, through 9 a.m. on Saturday, December 11th.

 

Granville Homes would like to remind you to take extra measures to prevent frost damage from occurring to your home this season. Frozen water pipes and sprinkler valves can create leaks and unnecessary damage to your home. Follow these simple steps to keep your home safeguarded this winter.

 

There are two types of products available to insulate your exterior pipes. They come in the form of foam sticks or fiberglass wrapping tape. Either method will work fine; remember that these products are not UV stable and will need to be replaced annually. All parts are readily available at your local home improvement center.

Take steps to minimize frozen pipes this winter:

Method #1:
Pipe Wrap Insulation

  • Materials: Pipe Wrap Insulation 3" wide by 1" thick (made by Thermwell Frost King); pipe wrap tape.
  • Instructions: Wrap insulation around the exposed pipe and hose bibb, cut off excess insulation, and secure with pipe wrap tape.

Method #2:
1" Foam Tubular Pipe Insulation

  • Materials: 1" Foam tubular pipe insulation (6' length) cut to fit; pipe wrap tape.
  • Instructions: Cut foam pipe insulation to correct length, wrap around the pipe and hose bibb, and secure with tape.

If you cannot make it to the home improvement store before the first freeze, you can wrap a large towel around your pipes. Ensure the entire pipe and hose bibb are covered with several layers of material. You may need to add duct tape to secure the towel in place. This method is only a temporary fix, and we recommend you utilize method #1 or #2 above for a long-term solution.
